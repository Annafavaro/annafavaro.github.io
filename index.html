About

I am a Postdoctoral Research Fellow at Harvard Medical School and Massachusetts General Hospital. I earned my PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Center for Language and Speech Processing (CLSP) at The Johns Hopkins University. My research focuses on developing digital biomarkers and multimodal models for neurodegenerative disease detection and monitoring using speech, eye movements, handwriting, and wearable sensing. Outside of work, I enjoy philosophy, fashion, and long walks in the nature.

Machine Learning for Medical Applications — EN.520.439

View course description

I served as a teaching assistant for Machine Learning for Medical Applications in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at The Johns Hopkins University.

Catalog description: EN.520.439. Machine Learning for Medical Applications. 3 credits.

In this course, students learn the basic principles of artificial intelligence and machine learning techniques applied to medical applications, as well as medical concepts common in healthcare environments.

The course covers different types of biomedical signals, including electroencephalograms, electrocardiograms, sound, medical imaging, and their associated processing methodologies. The primary objective is to give students the tools needed to develop new artificial-intelligence-related ideas in biomedical environments.

At the end of the course, students apply their newly acquired knowledge to complete a cumulative final project dealing with a real-world situation.

Credits: 3 Department: Electrical and Computer Engineering Distribution area: Engineering
